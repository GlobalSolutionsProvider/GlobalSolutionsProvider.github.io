History & Politics

  1. Founding: The United States was founded on July 4, 1776, with the signing of the Declaration of Independence.

  2. Constitution: The U.S. Constitution, adopted in 1787, is the supreme law of the land.

  3. Bill of Rights: The first ten amendments to the Constitution, known as the Bill of Rights, guarantee fundamental freedoms and rights.

  4. Civil War: The Civil War (1861-1865) was fought over issues including states' rights and slavery.

  5. Emancipation Proclamation: Issued by President Abraham Lincoln in 1863, it declared the freedom of all enslaved people in Confederate territory.

  6. Women's Suffrage: Women gained the right to vote in 1920 with the 19th Amendment.

  7. Civil Rights Movement: The 1960s saw major civil rights advancements, including the Civil Rights Act of 1964 and the Voting Rights Act of 1965.

  8. Presidency: The U.S. has had 46 presidents, with Joe Biden being the current president (as of 2024).

  9. Branches of Government: The U.S. government is divided into three branches: Executive, Legislative, and Judicial.

  10. Federal System: The U.S. operates under a federal system, dividing powers between the national government and the states.
